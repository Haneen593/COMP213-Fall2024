Who We Are?

         
                   

Wendy's is an internationally recognized fast-food restaurant chain famous for its hamburgers, fresh ingredients, and unique menu offerings. It was founded by Dave Thomas on November 15, 1969, in Columbus, Ohio. Dave named the restaurant after his fourth child, Melinda Lou "Wendy" Thomas, affectionately nicknamed Wendy. From the beginning, Wendy's stood out by offering high-quality, fresh, never-frozen beef for its burgers, setting a standard in the fast-food industry. The chain also became known for its signature Frosty dessert, a creamy blend of chocolate and vanilla, which remains a menu staple. In 1970, Wendy’s introduced the first modern drive-thru system, the "Pick-Up Window," revolutionizing customer convenience. Over time, the brand expanded globally, opening thousands of locations and becoming a favorite for its consistent quality and innovative items like the Baconator and Spicy Chicken Sandwich. Wendy’s is also known for its humorous and engaging marketing campaigns, particularly on social media, which have helped solidify its place in popular culture. Today, Wendy’s continues to focus on delivering fresh, high-quality meals while maintaining the values and vision of its founder, Dave Thomas.
